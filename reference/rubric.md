# Career Compass Fit Rubric

## 2.1 HQ Geography (in-scope)

- Spanish-speaking Latin America (all countries)
- Spain
- Brazil
- Portugal

**Excluded:** All other geographies, including US/EU companies with Spanish-speaking leadership but non-LatAm/Iberian HQ.

**Reasoning:** Strict (LatAm Spanish-speaking + Spain only) is too narrow — agent would exhaust the universe within months. Broad (Italy, bilingual US/EU) dilutes the bilingual-bridge thesis. Brazil included because Portuguese is conversational for Wendy and business-context cultural overlap with Spanish-speaking LatAm is high. Portugal included for the same reason; also, Lusophone employers often underserved by Tokyo bilingual talent market.

## 2.2 Asia Operations

| Tier | Definition | Status |
|------|------------|--------|
| A | Registered Japan entity (KK, GK, branch) with local staff | **In-scope** |
| B | APAC regional hub anywhere in Asia, with Japan as covered market | **In-scope** |
| C | Active commercial presence in Japan, exploring entry, no permanent entity | **Watch list** (separate output category) |
| D | Export-only, no on-the-ground Asia presence | Out of scope |

**Output requirement:** Each surfaced company is explicitly tagged A / B / C in the email and CSV.

**Reasoning:** Tier B included because some of the most interesting roles for Wendy's profile (regional commercial roles, APAC trade marketing) sit in Singapore-headquartered regional offices but are open to Tokyo-based hires. Tier C kept separate because signal quality is much lower — these are conversation-starters, not near-term opportunities — and lumping them with A/B would dilute the actionable list.

## 2.3 Industry Weighting

**High weight** (direct experience):
- Logistics, shipping, freight
- Aviation, airlines, aerospace (incl. supplier/MRO)
- Travel, hospitality, tourism
- Retail, home improvement, consumer durables
- ICT, enterprise software, B2B technology services
- Food, agribusiness, protein, beverages

**Medium weight** (adjacent — skills transfer with moderate ramp-up):
- CPG / FMCG
- E-commerce and marketplaces
- Industrial trading houses, B2B distribution
- Professional services, consulting
- Wine, spirits, premium goods exports
- Automotive and auto parts

**Lower weight** (further from background, not excluded):
- Heavy manufacturing, machinery, construction equipment
- Pharma, medical devices, biotech
- Finance, banking, insurance, fintech
- Energy, renewables
- Mining, metals
- Chemicals

**Excluded entirely:**
- Defense, military contractors
- Gambling
- Adult industries
- Tobacco

**Reasoning:** Industry-agnostic ignores the reality that hiring managers often want industry-specific candidates. Tight focus list locks in too early before Wendy's center of gravity is settled. Weighted approach lets the agent surface broadly but rank by adjacency. Food/protein placed at high weight (rather than medium) because of Agrosuper deep-dive — knowledge has short shelf life if not used.

## 2.4 Company Size

All three tiers in scope, **tagged separately in output**:
- **Large:** ~5,000+ employees (established multinationals)
- **Mid:** ~200–5,000 employees (regional ambition)
- **Small:** <200 employees (often "first Japan hire" situations)

## 2.5 Role Archetypes

**Core fit:**
- Trade Marketing Manager / Specialist (Asia, APAC, Japan)
- Commercial Manager / Country Manager Japan
- Business Development Manager APAC or Japan
- Sales Manager Japan (B2B contexts)
- Key Account Manager Japan / Asia
- Marketing Manager Japan / APAC

**Strong adjacent fit:**
- Product Manager (commercial / category context)
- Pricing Manager / Revenue Manager
- Category Manager
- Channel Manager
- Partnership Manager / Alliance Manager APAC
- Procurement / Sourcing Manager

**Possible fit:**
- Operations Manager Japan (commercial-leaning, not pure logistics ops)
- Project Manager (commercial transformation projects)
- Executive Assistant to senior LatAm/Spanish executive (especially first-Japan-hire / Chief of Staff hybrid roles at small companies)
- Customer Success Manager APAC (B2B contexts)

**Out of scope:**
- Pure marketing communications / brand / PR / creative
- Pure operations or supply chain ops
- Engineering, data science, software development
- Finance roles requiring CPA / CFA / equivalent licensure

## 2.6 Disqualifiers

**Hard disqualifiers** (never surface):
- Requires relocation outside Tokyo / Japan
- Requires native-level Japanese (legal, government-facing, native-customer-facing roles)
- Industries in the "Excluded entirely" list above
- Companies under active major scandal

**Soft disqualifiers** (surface but flag prominently):
- Roles requiring specific technical credentials Wendy doesn't have
- Companies with consistent recent poor reputation reviews specific to Japan office
- Companies in active major Tokyo office downsizing
- Country Manager / Japan-public-facing roles where near-native Japanese is implicitly required
